Dal Tadka

Dal Tadka is a popular Indian lentil dish made with arhar dal (husked & split pigeon pea lentils) or masoor dal (husked & split red lentils). This Dal Tadka recipe gives you a delicious creamy dal with smoked flavors.

Ingridents

For cooking lentils

  • 0.5 cup tuvar dal (arhar dal or split pigeon pea lentils) or 200 grams or ½ cup tuvar dal + ½ cup masoor dal (split pink lentils)
  • 0.5 to 1 green chilies or serrano peppers – chopped or slit lengthwise
  • 0.25 cup chopped onions or 1 medium sized onion
  • 0.5 cup finely chopped tomatoes or 2 medium sized tomatoes
  • 0.5 teaspoon finely chopped ginger or 1 inch ginger, peeled and finely chopped
  • 0.25 teaspoon turmeric powder (ground turmeric)
  • 0.25 teaspoon Garam Masala
  • 0.5 pinch asafoetida (hing) – optional
  • 1.25 cups water – for pressure cooking
  • 0.5 or 1 tablespoon low-fat cream – optional
  • 1 tablespoon chopped coriander leaves (cilantro)
  • salt as required

For cooking Tadka

  • 1.5 tablespoon oil or 3 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter)
  • 0.5 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2.5 to 3 medium garlic cloves – finely chopped
  • 1 to 1.5 dry red chilies – broken and seeds removed
  • a generous pinch of asafoetida (hing) – optional
  • 0.5 teaspoon crushed kasuri methi (dry fenugreek leaves) – optional
  • 0.25 teaspoon red chili powder or cayenne pepper or paprika
  • For Garnish :- 0.5 tablespoon chopped coriander leaves (cilantro)

Instructions

Cooking Dal

  1. Thoroughly rinse 1 cup tuvar dal (arhar dal, or split & husked pigeon pea lentils) in water. Add them to a 3-litre pressure cooker. No pressure cooker? You can also cook the lentils in a pot. Soak the lentils for an hour or two before you cook them in a pot.
  2. Add ½ cup chopped onions, 1 cup finely chopped tomatoes, 1 or 2 green chilies (1 teaspoon of chopped Anaheim pepper or Serrano pepper can be subbed for green chillies) and 1 teaspoon finely chopped ginger.
  3. Pour 2.5 cups of water into the pressure cooker. If cooking in a pot, then about 4 to 4.5 cups of water can be added.
  4. Add ½ turmeric powder and 1 pinch of asafoetida (hing). If you do not have asafoetida, then skip it.Mix Well
  5. Pressure cook the lentils for about 7-8 whistles, or until they become soft and creamy. Mash the cooked lentils with a wired whisk or with a spoon and keep aside. If the consistency looks thick, then add about ½ – 1 cup water (depending on the thickness) to get a medium consistency. Simmer dal for 3 to 4 minutes.
  6. Once the desired consistency is reached, add 1-2 tablespoons low-fat cream (optional), ½ teaspoon garam masala powder, 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves (cilantro) and salt to taste. Switch off the the heat.
  7. Mix well so that everything combines uniformly. Taste and adjust salt before adding tadka

For Tadka

  1. Next, heat 3 tablespoons oil or ghee (clarified butter) in a small pan over low-medium heat. First, add 1 teaspoon cumin seeds and crackle them. The cumin should get fried and not be raw, but be careful not burn them.
  2. Now add 2 to 3 dry red chilies, a generous pinch of asafoetida, and 5-6 medium sized garlic cloves which have been finely chopped. Let the garlic brown lightly and the red chilies change color. Do not burn the garlic.
  3. Lastly, add 1 teaspoon crushed kasuri methi (fenugreek seeds) and ½ teaspoon red chili powder or cayenne pepper. Mix well and turn off the stove.
  4. Pour entire tempering along with the oil or ghee into the dal.
  5. You can either mix the dal with tempering, or serve the dal tadka with the tempering on top it. I personally like the aesthetic of having the tadka floating on top. Garnish with coriander leaves. Enjoy!
  6. Serving Suggestion: Serve dal tadka hot with steamed basmati rice or jeera rice (cumin rice), or with roti, naan or paratha.
